What exists right now

A fully working training app — built for climbers

BetaLog is a free web app that climbers use to track their sessions, log grades, run hangboard timers, follow training routines, and get AI coaching based on their actual training history.

It runs entirely in the browser — no app store, no install. Climbers add it to their home screen and it behaves like a native app. It works offline, which matters because phone signal at climbing walls is often poor.

Right now it stores everything locally on the user's device. The next step is adding cloud accounts — which is where the gym partnership becomes relevant.

The gap in the market

Most indoor climbing gyms have no training tool to offer their members beyond generic fitness apps that know nothing about climbing. Vertical Life and The Crag cover outdoor route databases. Nobody has nailed the training + gym integration + social combination for indoor climbing. That is the space BetaLog is building into.

How it works end-to-end

The member journey

Here is what the experience looks like for one of your members, from joining your gym to booking a coaching session — all through the app.

1
Member joins your gym

Your existing member management system (Rock Gym Pro or similar) automatically triggers a BetaLog invitation to their email. Their account is pre-set up with your gym attached.

2
They open the app

They see your gym's current route board, your coaches, and any active challenges you've set. Their grade logging now maps against real problems on your walls.

3
They train and log sessions

Every visit auto-creates a session stub when they check in. They log what they climbed. Over weeks, the app builds a picture of their progress and training patterns.

4
AI coach spots a plateau

The coach notices their send rate at V5 has stalled for three weeks and their gym visits have dropped. It suggests a technique session would help bridge the gap.

5
One tap to book

A "Book with Sarah — Technique specialist" button appears in the coach output, linking directly to your booking page. The insight drives a real commercial outcome.

6
You see the engagement data

In your gym dashboard you can see how many coaching bookings came via the app, member streak trends, and which grade ranges are most active — without seeing individual personal data.

Longer-term opportunity

Owning the platform — or selling it

Option A — A gym buys it and runs it

A forward-thinking gym owner acquires BetaLog outright — the codebase, brand, and user base. They become the operator and sell access to other gyms as a SaaS product. They own the platform their competitors have to pay to use.

This is not hypothetical — gym management software companies started exactly this way. Rock Gym Pro, the dominant UK/US gym management system, began as a tool built by a climber for one gym and expanded from there.

Option B — Sell to a larger player

Once the platform has meaningful user numbers and a handful of paying gym partners, it becomes an attractive acquisition target for:

Gym management companies (Rock Gym Pro, Mindbody, Onsight Pro) looking to add a member-facing training layer to their existing back-office systems.

Climbing brands (Lattice Training, Tension Climbing, Moon Climbing) looking for a direct digital channel to climbers.

Fitness tech companies looking to expand into vertical sports.

The acquisition value is driven by three things: active user count, recurring gym revenue, and the quality of the member data. All three grow together as the platform scales.

Option C — Stay independent and grow

Neither sell nor acquire — take on the gym partner role, use that relationship to accelerate development, and build a UK climbing tech business. Slower but the upside is entirely yours.

The founder's advantage

A gym owner who comes in as a partner at this stage gets terms that will never be available again — founding pricing locked permanently, direct input on the product roadmap, and the option to acquire before the product has been priced by market demand. The time to have that conversation is now, not after the first ten gyms are signed up.
